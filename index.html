Bio

I am a research scientist and PhD student in mathematics working at the intersection of machine learning, representation learning, graph neural networks, and mathematical structures. My current research focuses on using neural representations to study knot invariants, topological structure, and scientific problems where geometry and data interact.

My work combines theoretical interests in mathematics with applied experience in data science, AI systems, and scientific machine learning. I am particularly interested in how modern machine learning models reveal, compress, or fail to capture structure in complex mathematical and scientific objects.

Professional Experience

My professional path has moved between industry data science, applied machine learning, and scientific research. I began as a data analyst at Nielsen, later led data science teams in the financial and consumer industries at Banregio and Heineken, and then worked at Schneider Electric on global AI initiatives for procurement, price prediction, supplier intelligence, and graph-based anomaly detection. I am currently a Research Scientist at Oxford Immune Algorithmics, where my work focuses on AI for science, scientific machine learning, and representation learning for biological and mathematical structures.
